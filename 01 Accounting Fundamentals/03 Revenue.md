# 03. Revenue

## Learning Objectives

After completing this topic, you should be able to:

- Define revenue.
- Differentiate revenue from profit and cash.
- Understand why revenue is important.
- Identify different types of revenue.

---

# Definition

Revenue is the income a company earns from its normal business activities, such as selling products or providing services.

It is often called the **Top Line** because it appears at the top of the Income Statement.

---

# Why It Matters

Revenue indicates the size and growth of a business.

Investors and analysts monitor revenue because consistent growth often signals increasing demand and business expansion.

However, high revenue does not necessarily mean high profitability.

---

# Key Concepts

### Revenue vs Profit

- **Revenue** is the total amount earned from sales.
- **Profit** is what remains after deducting all expenses.

Example:

Revenue = €100,000

Expenses = €75,000

Profit = €25,000

---

### Revenue vs Cash

Revenue is **earned**.

Cash is **received**.

A company may recognize revenue before receiving payment if the sale is made on credit.

---

### Types of Revenue

**Operating Revenue**

Revenue generated from the company's primary business activities.

Example:
A car manufacturer selling vehicles.

---

**Non-Operating Revenue**

Revenue generated from activities outside the company's core business.

Examples:

- Interest income
- Rental income
- Investment gains

---

# Real-Life Example

Apple sells an iPhone for €1,200.

The €1,200 is recognized as revenue when Apple fulfills its performance obligation (generally when the customer takes control of the product), even if payment was made using a credit card and Apple receives the cash later through the payment network.

---

# Financial Statement Impact

Revenue appears at the top of the **Income Statement**.

Revenue itself is not recorded on the Balance Sheet, but if payment has not yet been received, the company records an **Accounts Receivable** asset.

---

# Common Interview Questions

### Is revenue the same as profit?

No.

Revenue is the total income earned before expenses.

---

### Can a company have high revenue but lose money?

Yes.

If expenses exceed revenue, the company reports a loss.

---

### Is revenue always cash?

No.

Revenue can be recognized before cash is received.

---

# Common Misconceptions

❌ Revenue equals profit.

False.

Profit is calculated after deducting expenses.

---

❌ Higher revenue always means a better company.

False.

Profitability and cash flow are equally important.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Revenue is income earned from normal business operations.
- Revenue appears at the top of the Income Statement.
- Revenue is different from profit and cash.
- Revenue growth is important but should be analyzed alongside profitability.
